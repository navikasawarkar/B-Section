Tribute to Swatantra Veer Savarkar

Vinayak Damodar Savarkar

Vinayak Damodar Savarkar, also known as Swatantryaveer Savarkar, was a prominent Indian freedom fighter, politician, and social reformer, born in 1883. He is credited with coining the term Hindutva and was a key figure in the Hindu Mahasabha, a Hindu nationalist organization. Savarkar's life was marked by both revolutionary activism and later collaborations with the British, as well as his strong belief in a united India.

"One country, one God, one caste, one mind, brothers, all of us without difference, without doubt."

Timeline of Achievements

  • Born: May 28, 1883, in Bhagur village, Nashik district, Maharashtra.
  • Graduated:Degree (BA) from Fergusson College, Pune, in 1905.
  • Lawyer: Gray's Inn in London.
  • Joined:Hindu Mahasabha after his release from Ratnagiri in 1937.
  • President:He became the President of the organization and served for about 7 years.
  • Motive:Unity in nation
  • Passed Away:February 26, 1966, at the age of 83.
